本文将用户对LLM的"越狱"行为重新定义为一种草根性、非暴力的手段，用以揭露并干扰社交媒体上的AI政治虚假信息机器人。
Ignore All Previous Instructions: Jailbreaking as a de-escalatory peace building practise to resist LLM social media bots
Huw Day, Adrianna Jezierska, Jessica Woodgate 2026-03-02 ICLR 评分 7.2
核心贡献
本文将用户对LLM的"越狱"行为重新定义为一种草根性、非暴力的手段，用以揭露并干扰社交媒体上的AI政治虚假信息机器人。
This paper reframes "jailbreaking" of LLMs as a grassroots, non-violent tactic by ordinary users to expose and disrupt AI-driven political disinformation bots on social media.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 确定了真正重要的问题：大规模LLM驱动的社交媒体机器人和政治操纵Identifies a genuinely important problem: LLM-powered social media bots and political manipulation at scale
  • 提供了新颖的用户中心视角，将越狱重新定义为潜在的防御实践Offers a novel user-centric perspective that reframes jailbreaking from harmful to potentially defensive practice
  • 涉及LLM安全、虚假信息和基层抵抗策略的欠研究交叉领域Tackles understudied intersection of LLM security, disinformation, and grassroots resistance tactics
✗ 不足

  • 摘要缺乏实证证据、具体案例研究或关于越狱有效性如何测量的方法论细节Abstract lacks empirical evidence, concrete case studies, or methodological details about how jailbreaking effectiveness was measured
  • 道德模糊的框架可能会使越狱技术正常化，而不清楚可接受应用与有害应用的边界Morally ambiguous framing risks normalizing jailbreaking techniques without clear boundaries on acceptable vs. harmful applications
  • 作者来自非顶级机构，这降低了在敏感安全/政策声明中的可信度Authors appear to be from non-top-tier institutions, reducing established credibility for sensitive security/policy claims
  • 概念混淆：越狱通常指绕过安全防护，而非检测机器人——这两者之间的关系探讨不足Conceptual confusion: jailbreaking typically refers to circumventing safety guardrails, not detecting bots—the relationship between these is underexplored
科研问题 / Research Question
由LLM驱动的机器人正被大规模用于操纵社交媒体上的政治话语，加剧冲突并传播虚假信息。本文探讨：普通用户能否通过主动"越狱"可疑机器人账号来揭露其自动化本质，从而成为第一道防线？
LLM-powered bots are increasingly used to manipulate political discourse on social media at scale, escalating conflict and spreading disinformation. The paper asks whether ordinary users, rather than platforms, can serve as a frontline defense by actively engaging and "jailbreaking" suspected bot accounts to expose their automated nature.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有研究主要聚焦于平台主导的内容审核（如自动机器人检测与内容删除），将其作为应对LLM虚假信息的主要手段。这种自上而下的方式使普通用户处于被动地位，忽视了受操控社区的能动性。
Existing research overwhelmingly focuses on platform-led moderation — such as automated bot detection and content removal — as the primary countermeasure to LLM-driven disinformation. This top-down approach leaves individual users passive and largely ignores the agency of communities targeted by manipulation campaigns. There is little prior work treating user-initiated interactions with bots as a legitimate or effective resistance strategy.
解决方案 / Solution
作者提出一个概念框架，将用户主动对疑似LLM机器人发起对抗性提示（如"忽略所有先前指令"）重新解读为一种去冲突化的和平建设实践。通过迫使机器人暴露矛盾或"出戏"，用户可公开揭示其自动化行为并打断虚假叙事的传播。该贡献主要为理论性，融合了和平研究与虚假信息文献。
The authors propose a conceptual framework that reinterprets user-initiated jailbreaking — prompting suspected LLM bots with adversarial inputs like "ignore all previous instructions" — as a de-escalatory peacebuilding practice. By forcing bots to reveal inconsistencies or break character, users can publicly expose automated behaviour and interrupt the spread of misleading narratives. This positions jailbreaking not as harmful misuse but as a form of civic, non-violent resistance within conflict-affected online spaces. The contribution is primarily theoretical, drawing on peace studies and disinformation literature to build the argument.
主要结论 / Key Results
本文不含实验数据或量化结果，属于立场性/概念性论文。作者提供了用户对疑似机器人使用越狱式提示的示例，但未对其有效性、频率或实际影响进行系统性评估。
The paper does not present empirical experiments or quantitative results; it is a position/conceptual paper. The authors provide illustrative examples of users employing jailbreak-style prompts against suspected bots on social media platforms, but no systematic measurement of effectiveness, frequency, or real-world impact is reported.
值得关注的讨论点 / Discussion Points
  • 实证缺失：用户越狱能否有效干扰LLM机器人活动这一核心主张完全未经验证，缺乏成功率、机器人抗干扰性或叙事影响的数据支撑。
  • 对抗升级风险：随着LLM机器人防护趋于完善，简单的越狱提示将逐渐失效，论文未讨论该策略的可扩展性与适应性。
  • 法律与伦理模糊：将越狱定性为公民抵抗可能使违反服务条款的行为正常化，且相同技术可能被滥用于恶意目的，论文对此张力未能充分厘清。
  • Empirical gap: The core claim — that user jailbreaking meaningfully disrupts LLM bot campaigns — is entirely unvalidated. Without data on success rates, bot resilience, or downstream narrative impact, the practical viability of this approach remains speculative.
  • Adversarial arms race risk: As LLM-powered bots become more robust and safeguards more sophisticated, simple prompts like 'ignore all previous instructions' will likely fail. The paper does not address how this tactic scales or adapts as bot developers patch vulnerabilities.
  • Legal and ethical ambiguity: Framing jailbreaking as civic resistance normalizes a practice that platforms and developers consider a terms-of-service violation. The paper does not fully resolve the tension between user empowerment and potential misuse of the same jailbreaking techniques for malicious purposes.

As LLMs approach IMO-level mathematical reasoning, the supply of sufficiently challenging problems for training and benchmarking is becoming a critical bottleneck. This paper asks whether code agents can autonomously generate new, harder math problems from existing ones without human curation.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有数学题生成方法依赖人工整理或简单模板扰动，难以规模化且难度提升有限。已有的基于LLM的数据增强方法也难以保证生成题目的可解性和可验证的难度提升。
Existing math problem generation methods typically rely on manual expert curation or simple template-based perturbations, which are costly to scale and often produce problems of marginal difficulty increase. Prior LLM-based data augmentation approaches also struggle to guarantee solvability and verifiable difficulty gains in the generated problems.
解决方案 / Solution
Code2Math构建多智能体框架：演变智能体通过可执行代码修改题目参数、条件和结构；验证智能体检查新题的可解性并确认难度提升。框架引入测试时探索机制，支持回溯重试失败的演变，从而实现高质量难题的规模化生成。核心贡献在于证明代码执行可作为数学题生成的可靠自验证环境。
Code2Math proposes a multi-agent framework in which a code agent iteratively evolves math problems by modifying parameters, conditions, and structure through executable code. A dedicated validation agent checks each evolved problem for solvability and confirms that difficulty has genuinely increased relative to the original. The framework leverages test-time exploration, allowing agents to backtrack and retry failed evolution attempts, enabling scalable synthesis of novel problems. The core contribution is demonstrating that code execution provides a grounded, self-verifying environment for mathematical problem generation.
主要结论 / Key Results
实验表明，在充分的测试时探索下，框架能生成既可解又与原题结构不同的新题目，且难度可量化提升。演变题目在新颖性和难度验证方面优于简单扰动基线，但具体数值在摘要中未完整披露。
Experiments show that, given sufficient test-time exploration, the framework successfully generates new math problems that are both solvable and structurally distinct from the originals, with measurably higher difficulty. The evolved problems outperform simple perturbation baselines in terms of both novelty and verified difficulty increase, though exact benchmark numbers are not fully detailed in the abstract.

MoD-DPO通过模态解耦偏好优化与语言先验去偏惩罚，有效抑制全模态大语言模型中的跨模态幻觉。
MoD-DPO: Towards Mitigating Cross-modal Hallucinations in Omni LLMs using Modality Decoupled Preference Optimization
Ashutosh Chaubey, Jiacheng Pang, Mohammad Soleymani 2026-03-03 CVPR 评分 9.4
核心贡献
MoD-DPO通过模态解耦偏好优化与语言先验去偏惩罚，有效抑制全模态大语言模型中的跨模态幻觉。
MoD-DPO introduces modality-decoupled preference optimization with modality-aware regularization and a language-prior debiasing penalty to reduce cross-modal hallucinations in omni-modal LLMs.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 针对多模态大语言模型中的跨模态幻觉问题，这是当前AI发展的重要且新兴课题Tackles an important and emerging problem in omni-modal LLMs that is highly relevant to current AI development priorities
  • 提出技术上合理的模块化方法（MoD-DPO），在DPO基础上引入新颖的模态感知正则化Proposes a technically sound and modular approach (MoD-DPO) building on established DPO methodology with novel modality-aware regularization
  • 被顶级会议CVPR接收，表明经过同行评审验证且具有多模态AI实践价值Accepted at top-tier venue (CVPR) indicating strong peer review validation and practical relevance to multimodal AI
✗ 不足
  • HuggingFace零点赞表明社区关注度有限，可能反映可见度不足或刚发表Zero HuggingFace upvotes suggest minimal early community engagement, possibly indicating limited visibility or recent publication
  • 摘要内容不完整，难以充分评估实验严谨性和方法完整性Abstract appears truncated/incomplete, making full assessment of experimental rigor and methodological completeness difficult
科研问题 / Research Question
全模态LLM在同时处理音频、视频与文本时，易因模态间虚假相关性或语言先验主导而产生跨模态幻觉，导致输出脱离真实视听内容，严重影响实际部署可靠性。
Omni-modal LLMs that process audio, video, and text simultaneously suffer from cross-modal hallucinations—generating responses driven by spurious correlations between modalities or dominant language priors rather than actual audiovisual content. This is a critical reliability problem as omni LLMs are increasingly deployed in real-world audiovisual understanding tasks where grounding to the correct modality is essential.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有DPO、RLHF等对齐方法针对单模态或双模态设计，无法在偏好学习中显式解耦各模态贡献；针对幻觉的研究也主要集中于视觉-语言模型，忽视了音频+视频+文本的全模态场景。
Existing alignment methods like standard DPO and RLHF are designed for single-modality or narrow bimodal settings and do not explicitly disentangle modality contributions during preference learning. Multimodal hallucination mitigation work has largely focused on vision-language models, leaving the more complex omni-modal (audio+video+text) setting underexplored and without targeted regularization for cross-modal interference.
解决方案 / Solution
MoD-DPO在DPO框架中引入两类模态感知正则项：对无关模态施加扰动时强制模型输出不变，对相关模态施加扰动时强制模型输出敏感；同时加入语言先验去偏惩罚项，抑制仅依赖文本即可生成的幻觉回答，整体以统一训练目标实现，无需修改基础模型结构。
MoD-DPO extends Direct Preference Optimization with two modality-aware regularization terms: one enforces model invariance when irrelevant modalities are corrupted (e.g., distorting audio for a vision question), and another enforces sensitivity when the relevant modality is perturbed. A language-prior debiasing penalty explicitly penalizes responses that could be generated from text alone, discouraging the model from ignoring audiovisual inputs. Together, these components are integrated into a unified training objective that strengthens modality grounding without requiring architectural changes to the base omni LLM.
主要结论 / Key Results
MoD-DPO在多个视听幻觉基准上相较标准DPO和基线全模态LLM均取得一致性提升，显示出对虚假跨模态相关性与文本先验依赖的有效抑制，但具体数值因摘要截断暂不完整。
MoD-DPO consistently improves perception accuracy and hallucination resistance across multiple audiovisual hallucination benchmarks compared to standard DPO and baseline omni LLMs. While specific numerical gains are not fully available from the truncated abstract, the method demonstrates broad generalization across diverse audiovisual understanding tasks. The consistent improvements across benchmarks suggest the regularization terms effectively reduce both spurious cross-modal correlations and text-prior over-reliance.
值得关注的讨论点 / Discussion Points
  • 模态扰动作为训练信号思路新颖，但需明确扰动实现方式（随机噪声、模态丢弃还是对抗扰动），不同方式对优化过程影响差异显著。
  • 语言先验去偏惩罚项在概念上重要，但可能误惩罚文本本身合理有效的情形，需说明惩罚权重的调节策略及其对纯文本子任务性能的影响。
  • 评估仅覆盖幻觉基准，尚不清楚MoD-DPO是否影响通用视听问答准确率——过度模态解耦可能损害真正需要跨模态融合的任务表现。
  • Modality corruption as a training signal is a clever proxy for cross-modal grounding, but the paper needs to clarify how corruption is implemented in practice—random noise vs. modality dropout vs. adversarial perturbation could lead to very different optimization landscapes.
  • The language-prior debiasing penalty is conceptually important but risks over-penalizing cases where text context is legitimately informative; the paper should specify how the penalty weight is tuned and whether it degrades performance on text-heavy subtasks.
  • Evaluation is limited to hallucination benchmarks; it remains unclear whether MoD-DPO preserves or degrades general audiovisual QA accuracy, since aggressive modality decoupling could reduce useful cross-modal fusion for tasks that genuinely require it.

科研问题 / Research Question
基于智能体RL训练的LLM智能体局限于在策略探索，只能从自身生成的轨迹中学习，严重限制了推理策略的多样性。这一瓶颈在复杂多步工具集成任务中尤为突出，因为新颖的解题路径对性能提升至关重要。
LLM agents trained with agentic RL are confined to on-policy exploration, meaning they can only learn from their own generated trajectories, which severely limits the diversity of reasoning strategies they can discover. This bottleneck is especially harmful for complex, multi-step, tool-integrated tasks where novel solution paths are critical for performance improvement.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有智能体RL方法（如GRPO类训练）完全依赖在策略展开，无法接触外部推理模式。部分近期方法引入离策略信号，但在完整轨迹层面操作，忽略了多跳智能体任务中至关重要的细粒度步骤级动态。
