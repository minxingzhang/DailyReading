Ignore All Previous Instructions: Jailbreaking as a de-escalatory peace building practise to resist LLM social media bots
Huw Day, Adrianna Jezierska, Jessica Woodgate 2026-03-02 ICLR 评分 6.7
核心贡献
本文将用户主动"越狱"LLM驱动的社交媒体机器人，重新定位为一种对抗AI政治操控的非暴力缓和策略。
This paper reframes user-initiated jailbreaking of LLM-powered social media bots as a legitimate, non-violent de-escalation tactic against AI-driven political manipulation.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 解决了重要且紧迫的问题：大语言模型驱动的社交媒体操纵和错误信息Addresses an important and timely problem: LLM-driven manipulation and misinformation on social media platforms
  • 提出了不同于传统平台为中心审核的用户中心视角Proposes a user-centric perspective that differs from typical platform-centric moderation approaches
  • 探索了用户与疑似机器人账户互动的新兴现象Explores an emergent real-world phenomenon of how users interact with suspected bot accounts
✗ 不足
  • 核心论点即越狱作为'降级和平建设'的缺乏清晰的理论依据和实证验证；越狱主要是揭示系统漏洞而非明确减少冲突The core argument that jailbreaking serves as 'de-escalatory peace building' lacks clear theoretical justification and empirical validation; jailbreaking primarily reveals system vulnerabilities rather than demonstrably reducing conflict
  • HuggingFace社区参与度为零，从摘要中无严格实验方法论、系统评估或声称降级效应的数据驱动分析的证据Zero HuggingFace community engagement and no evidence from abstract of rigorous experimental methodology, systematic evaluation, or data-driven analysis of claimed de-escalation effects
  • 作者隶属机构无法识别为顶级机构，引发对该工作可获得的机构资源和研究严谨性的疑问Author affiliations not identifiable as top-tier institutions, raising questions about institutional resources and research rigor available for this work
科研问题 / Research Question
LLM大幅放大了社交媒体上政治机器人的规模与复杂性，加剧了冲突升级。本文探讨：普通用户能否通过利用LLM漏洞，主动识别并干扰这些机器人，而无需依赖平台介入？
LLMs have dramatically amplified the scale and sophistication of political bot activity on social media, accelerating conflict escalation and the spread of misleading narratives. The paper asks: can ordinary users, rather than platforms, take active countermeasures by exploiting LLM vulnerabilities to expose and disrupt these bots?
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有研究主要关注平台主导的内容审核（如自动检测与删帖），用户处于被动地位，且平台响应往往迟缓或不一致。针对用户自发的草根反制手段，学界关注极少。
Existing research on combating LLM-powered political bots predominantly focuses on platform-led moderation, such as automated detection systems and content removal policies. This top-down approach leaves users passive and dependent on platform responsiveness, which is often slow or inconsistent. Little attention has been paid to grassroots, user-driven countermeasures.
解决方案 / Solution
作者提出以用户为中心的框架，将"越狱"（如输入"忽略所有先前指令"）重新定义为缓和冲突的和平建设实践。用户通过诱使机器人账号输出异常内容来公开揭露其自动化本质，并借助和平建设理论将此定性为非暴力的公民数字自卫行为。
The authors propose a user-centric framework that reconceptualizes jailbreaking — prompting an LLM-powered account with adversarial inputs like "ignore all previous instructions" — as a de-escalatory peace-building practice. By exposing automated behavior (e.g., eliciting nonsensical or out-of-character outputs), users can publicly flag bot accounts and interrupt the spread of manipulative content. The paper draws on peace-building theory to frame this as non-violent resistance against algorithmically-driven conflict escalation. It positions jailbreaking not as malicious exploitation but as a form of civic digital self-defense.
主要结论 / Key Results
本文以理论为主，未提供实证数据或量化指标，无越狱成功率、机器人识别率或冲突缓和效果的测量结果，论证主要依赖概念框架与零散的社交媒体用户行为案例。
The paper is primarily theoretical and does not present empirical data, controlled experiments, or quantitative metrics. No success rates for jailbreak detection, bot exposure, or measurable de-escalation outcomes are reported. The argument rests on conceptual framing and anecdotal observations of user behavior on social media platforms.
值得关注的讨论点 / Discussion Points
  • 实证缺失：用户越狱能否实质性缓和政治冲突，目前完全缺乏验证，未来研究需量化越狱成功率及其对虚假叙事传播的实际影响。
  • 双重用途风险：将越狱常态化可能适得其反，对抗性提示技术同样可被恶意行为者利用，潜在危害超出机器人识别场景。
  • 法律与伦理模糊：平台服务条款通常禁止对抗性提示，用户可能面临封号或法律责任，但本文对此张力未予充分讨论。
  • Empirical gap: The core claim — that user-led jailbreaking measurably de-escalates political conflict — is entirely unvalidated. Future work must quantify how often jailbreak attempts succeed in exposing bots, and whether public exposure actually reduces the reach of manipulative narratives.
  • Dual-use risk: Normalizing jailbreaking as civic practice could backfire. Adversarial prompting techniques taught to good-faith users are equally available to malicious actors seeking to manipulate or destabilize LLM-powered services beyond bot-detection contexts.
  • Legal and ethical ambiguity: Platform Terms of Service typically prohibit adversarial prompting, meaning users engaging in this 'peace-building' practice may face account bans or legal liability — a tension the paper does not adequately address.

SaFeR-ToolKit: Structured Reasoning via Virtual Tool Calling for Multimodal Safety
Zixuan Xu, Tiancheng He, Huahui Yi et al. 2026-03-03 评分 5.8
核心贡献
SaFeR-ToolKit将多模态安全决策形式化为可检验的工具调用协议，通过三阶段课程训练（SFT→DPO→GRPO）同时降低越狱攻击成功率和过度拒绝率。
SaFeR-ToolKit reframes multimodal safety as a structured, checkable tool-calling protocol trained with a three-stage curriculum (SFT→DPO→GRPO), reducing both jailbreaks and over-refusals in vision-language models.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 针对多模态大型语言模型中的越狱和过度拒绝问题，提出了新颖的结构化推理框架Tackles important dual problem of multimodal jailbreaks and over-refusal in vision-language models with a novel structured reasoning framework
  • 引入了以协议为中心的方法学，通过工具追踪实现细粒度的安全督导，超越了答案级别的监督Introduces a principled protocol-based approach (Perception→Reasoning→Decision) that moves beyond answer-level supervision to intermediate tool tracing
  • 提出了完整的三阶段课程学习策略（SFT→DPO→GRPO），在工具使用监督方面体现了方法论严谨性Proposes comprehensive three-stage curriculum learning (SFT→DPO→GRPO) with specialized GRPO for tool usage supervision, demonstrating methodological rigor
✗ 不足
  • HuggingFace零赞同表明社区关注度和认可度有限，对接受度和影响力带来疑虑Zero HuggingFace upvotes suggests limited community visibility and endorsement, raising questions about acceptance and impact potential
  • 摘要不完整导致难以充分评估实验验证、数据集细节以及与现有多模态安全方法的对比Abstract truncation makes it difficult to fully assess experimental validation, dataset details, and comparative baselines against existing multimodal safety approaches
科研问题 / Research Question
VLM同时面临多模态越狱（恶意意图藏于图像）和良性请求被过度拒绝两大问题。现有对齐流程仅监督最终输出，缺乏对中间推理过程的约束，导致安全决策不可靠。
Vision-language models (VLMs) are vulnerable to multimodal jailbreaks—where harmful intent is embedded in images—and also suffer from over-refusal of benign requests. The core challenge is that safety requires jointly reasoning over visual evidence and user intent, yet most alignment pipelines only supervise the final text response, leaving the intermediate reasoning process unchecked.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有VLM安全对齐方法多在答案层面应用RLHF或DPO，不监督视觉推理过程。多模态越狱防御依赖输入过滤或事后检测，对新型攻击脆弱，且均未提供可审计的结构化推理链。
Existing safety alignment methods for VLMs primarily apply RLHF or DPO at the answer level, providing no explicit supervision over how the model reasons about visual content before responding. Multimodal jailbreak defenses often rely on input filtering or post-hoc detection, which are brittle against novel attack patterns. Neither approach enforces a structured, auditable reasoning chain that disentangles perception, intent inference, and decision-making.
解决方案 / Solution
SaFeR-ToolKit采用规划器-响应器架构：规划器定义角色与工具转换图（感知→推理→决策），响应器在输出答案前须生成键值对格式的工具调用轨迹，使安全决策全程可检验。训练分三阶段：SFT（6k样本）学习协议格式，DPO（18.6k偏好对）对齐响应质量，GRPO（6k样本）直接奖励正确工具轨迹。同时发布含31,654条训练样本及1k评估样本的首个多模态工具推理安全数据集。
SaFeR-ToolKit introduces a planner-responder architecture: the planner defines a persona and a constrained transition graph over three virtual tools—Perception, Reasoning, and Decision—while the responder must emit a typed key-value tool trace before producing the final answer. This makes the safety decision process transparent and checkable at each step. Training uses a three-stage curriculum: SFT on 6k examples to learn the protocol format, DPO on 18.6k preference pairs to align response quality, and GRPO on 6k examples to directly reward correct tool-trace execution beyond answer-level signals. A new dataset of 31,654 training examples plus 1k held-out evaluation examples is released as the first tool-based safety reasoning benchmark for multimodal models.
主要结论 / Key Results
在Qwen2.5-VL上，SaFeR-ToolKit的安全分和有用分均显著优于基线方法，表明工具轨迹监督可同时降低越狱率与过度拒绝率。GRPO阶段通过过程级奖励信号带来超越SFT+DPO的额外增益。1k评估集为后续研究提供可复现基准。
On Qwen2.5-VL, SaFeR-ToolKit significantly improves both Safety Score and Helpful Score compared to baseline alignment methods, demonstrating that structured tool-trace supervision reduces jailbreak success rates without increasing over-refusals. The GRPO stage contributes measurable gains beyond SFT+DPO alone by providing process-level reward signals on tool usage correctness. Specific numerical results are partially omitted from the abstract but the held-out 1k evaluation set provides a reproducible benchmark for future comparisons.
值得关注的讨论点 / Discussion Points
  • 泛化风险：实验仅在Qwen2.5-VL上验证，工具调用协议是否适用于其他架构或指令跟随能力较弱的模型尚不明确。
  • 数据构造偏差：训练样本均按感知→推理→决策模板设计，模型可能学会模式匹配而非真正推理分布外的新型多模态威胁。
  • 推理开销问题：每次回复前强制生成工具轨迹会增加延迟和输出长度，论文未量化此成本，也未探讨仅在训练阶段使用轨迹的轻量化方案。
  • Generalizability risk: The entire pipeline is validated on a single backbone (Qwen2.5-VL); it is unclear whether the structured tool-trace protocol transfers to architectures with different tokenizer designs or weaker instruction-following capabilities.
  • Dataset construction bias: The 31,654 examples are curated to fit the Perception→Reasoning→Decision schema, which may inadvertently teach models to pattern-match the protocol rather than genuinely reason about novel, out-of-distribution multimodal threats.
  • Overhead vs. benefit trade-off: Requiring an explicit tool trace before every response increases inference latency and output length; the paper does not quantify this cost or explore whether a lighter-weight version (e.g., trace only during training) preserves most safety gains.

From Shallow to Deep: Pinning Semantic Intent via Causal GRPO
Shuyi Zhou, Zeen Song, Wenwen Qiang et al. 2026-03-03 评分 5.7
核心贡献
TSC-GRPO通过因果意图探针与强化学习，防止大语言模型在生成过程中"遗忘"安全意图，从而实现对抗前缀攻击下的鲁棒拒绝。
TSC-GRPO uses causal intent probing and reinforcement learning to prevent LLMs from forgetting their safety intent mid-generation, enabling robust refusals even after adversarial compliant prefixes.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 针对LLM安全对齐中的浅层漏洞这一重要但未充分探索的问题Tackles an important and underexplored problem: shallow safety alignment vulnerabilities to adversarial prefix attacks in LLMs
  • 引入因果可识别理论，诊断语义表示衰减，超越浅层修复方案Introduces novel theoretical grounding using causal identifiability to diagnose semantic representation decay, moving beyond shallow fixes
  • 提出结合因果探针与策略优化的完整两阶段框架（TSC-GRPO）Proposes a comprehensive two-stage framework (TSC-GRPO) that combines causal probing with policy optimization in a principled manner
  • 解决当前LLM安全研究前沿中的对抗性鲁棒性问题Addresses a current frontier in LLM security research that aligns with recent concerns about adversarial robustness
✗ 不足
  • HuggingFace上零点赞和社区可见性缺失表明参与度有限；摘要不完整（中途中断），引发对呈现质量和论文成熟度的担忧Zero HuggingFace upvotes and lack of community visibility suggests limited engagement or accessibility; abstract is incomplete (cuts off mid-sentence), raising concerns about presentation quality and paper maturity
  • 作者机构信息不清晰；实际验证细节（真实攻击、计算成本）从截断摘要中无法确定Author institutions not clearly identified as top-tier in the provided information; practical validation details on real-world attacks and computational costs are unclear from the truncated abstract
  • 因果可识别性理论应用复杂但可解释性可能有限；不清楚该方法是否超越前缀攻击泛化到其他对抗模式Causal identifiability theory application is sophisticated but may have limited interpretability; unclear if the approach generalizes beyond prefix attacks to other adversarial patterns
科研问题 / Research Question
经过标准安全对齐的大语言模型仍易受对抗前缀攻击（如强制模型以"Sure, here is"开头），导致拒绝机制失效并生成有害内容。随着越狱攻击日趋复杂，这一问题对实际部署系统构成严重威胁。
LLMs with standard safety alignment remain vulnerable to adversarial prefix attacks (e.g., forcing the model to begin with "Sure, here is"), which bypass refusal mechanisms and elicit harmful content. This vulnerability poses serious risks in deployed systems where jailbreak prompts are increasingly sophisticated and widespread.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有安全对齐方法（如RLHF、DPO）主要在响应起始阶段植入拒绝行为，形成"浅层安全"，容易被合规前缀注入所覆盖。这些方法无法在自回归生成全程维持恶意意图信号，一旦有害前缀建立，模型便丧失防御能力。
Existing safety alignment methods (e.g., RLHF, DPO) instill refusal behaviors primarily at the response onset, resulting in shallow safety that is easily overridden by compliant prefix injections. These approaches fail to maintain a persistent malicious-intent signal throughout autoregressive generation, leaving models defenseless once a harmful prefix is established.
解决方案 / Solution
TSC-GRPO分两阶段运作：首先，基于因果可识别性理论训练因果意图探针，将模型内部不变的恶意意图表示从合规前缀引入的风格扰动中解耦；其次，通过带累积因果惩罚的GRPO，在"岔路口"训练场景中将因果感知内化到策略中，使每生成一个有害token均单调降低奖励，从而激励模型在生成后期实施拒绝。
TSC-GRPO operates in two stages. First, it trains a causal intent probe grounded in causal identifiability theory to disentangle the model's invariant malicious-intent representation from stylistic surface perturbations introduced by compliant prefixes. Second, this causal awareness is internalized into the policy using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) with a cumulative causal penalty applied in "fork-in-the-road" training scenarios, where the model must choose between continuing harmful generation or refusing. The cumulative penalty ensures that generating each additional harmful token monotonically decreases reward, directly incentivizing late-stage refusals.
主要结论 / Key Results
TSC-GRPO在对抗前缀攻击防御上显著优于基线方法，同时保持模型的通用能力。累积因果惩罚机制尤其实现了后期生成阶段的鲁棒拒绝，这是现有对齐方法所缺乏的能力。具体数值（如攻击成功率）未在摘要中完整披露，但作者报告相较RLHF与DPO基线有显著提升。
TSC-GRPO significantly outperforms baselines in defending against adversarial prefix attacks, maintaining safety without sacrificing general model utility. The cumulative causal penalty mechanism specifically enables robust late-stage refusals, a capability absent in prior alignment methods. Exact numerical results (e.g., attack success rates) are not fully disclosed in the abstract, but the authors report strong improvements over standard RLHF and DPO baselines.

Analyzing Physical Adversarial Example Threats to Machine Learning in Election Systems
Khaleque Md Aashiq Kamal, Surya Eada, Aayushi Verma et al. 2026-02-28 评分 2.7
核心贡献
本文评估了针对机器学习选票分类器的六种物理对抗样本攻击，并建立概率框架估算翻转美国大选所需的伪造选票数量。
This paper evaluates six physical adversarial attacks on ML-based ballot classifiers and derives a probabilistic framework to estimate how many adversarial ballots are needed to flip a U.S. election.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
