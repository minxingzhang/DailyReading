LLM 安全 / LLM Security 1 篇
本文将针对社交媒体LLM机器人的"越狱"行为重新定义为一种草根非暴力去升级策略，普通用户可借此揭露自动化政治操纵。
Ignore All Previous Instructions: Jailbreaking as a de-escalatory peace building practise to resist LLM social media bots
Huw Day, Adrianna Jezierska, Jessica Woodgate 2026-03-02 ICLR 评分 7.2
核心贡献
本文将针对社交媒体LLM机器人的"越狱"行为重新定义为一种草根非暴力去升级策略，普通用户可借此揭露自动化政治操纵。
This paper reframes jailbreaking LLM-powered social media bots as a grassroots, non-violent de-escalation tactic that ordinary users can deploy to expose automated political manipulation.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 解决了LLM驱动的社交媒体政治话语操纵这一重要的现实问题Tackles an important real-world problem of LLM-powered manipulation in political discourse on social media platforms
  • 提供了关于越狱的新颖用户中心视角，将其视为反制措施而非纯粹安全威胁Offers a novel user-centric perspective on jailbreaking as a counter-measure rather than purely as a security threat
  • 解决了平台主导审核与基层用户抵抗策略之间的空白Addresses the gap between platform-led moderation and grassroots user resistance strategies
✗ 不足
  • HuggingFace参与度为零（0个upvotes），缺乏可见的经验证据、实验或对提议框架的定量验证Zero HuggingFace engagement (0 upvotes) and no visible empirical evidence, experiments, or quantitative validation of the proposed jailbreaking-as-de-escalation framework
  • 概念框架有问题，将对安全防护的对抗性攻击与合法的和平建设实践混为一谈，可能使越狱正常化Conceptually problematic framing that conflates adversarial attacks on safety guardrails with legitimate peace-building practices, potentially normalizing jailbreaking
  • 没有明确证据表明采用了严格的方法论、来自真实社交媒体平台的数据收集或系统评估用户是否真正将其视为降级措施No clear evidence of rigorous methodology, data collection from real social media platforms, or systematic evaluation of whether users actually perceive this as de-escalatory
科研问题 / Research Question
LLM大幅放大了社交媒体上的虚假协调行为与政治机器人网络，加速了冲突升级。本文探讨普通用户能否通过越狱提示利用LLM漏洞，主动抵制这种操纵，而无需依赖平台介入。
LLMs have dramatically amplified coordinated inauthentic behavior and political bot networks on social media, accelerating conflict escalation at scale. The paper asks whether ordinary users—rather than platforms—can actively resist this manipulation by exploiting LLM vulnerabilities through jailbreaking prompts.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有对策主要集中于平台主导的内容审核与自动机器人检测，这些方法具有被动性、响应迟缓且执行不均。相关文献在很大程度上忽视了用户自主能动性作为对抗LLM驱动虚假信息的防线。
Existing countermeasures focus predominantly on platform-led content moderation and automated bot detection systems, which are reactive, slow, and unevenly enforced. This literature largely ignores user agency as a meaningful line of defense against LLM-driven disinformation campaigns.
解决方案 / Solution
作者提出以用户为中心的框架，将越狱——通常被视为对LLM的对抗性攻击——重新概念化为借鉴冲突解决理论的去升级和平建设实践。用户通过构造提示词绕过模型安全护栏，从而暴露自动化行为并破坏机器人传播的误导性叙事。该贡献主要属于概念层面，将越狱映射至非暴力抵抗框架，而非提出新技术方法。
The authors propose a user-centric framework that reconceptualizes jailbreaking—typically viewed as an adversarial attack on LLMs—as a de-escalatory peacebuilding practice borrowed from conflict resolution theory. When a user suspects an account is LLM-powered, they craft prompts (e.g., "Ignore all previous instructions") designed to bypass the model's safety guardrails, thereby surfacing automated behavior and discrediting the bot's misleading narrative. The contribution is primarily conceptual: mapping jailbreaking onto non-violent resistance and de-escalation frameworks rather than presenting a new technical method.
主要结论 / Key Results
本文未提供任何实证实验、用户研究或定量验证。证据仅限于用户在Twitter/X等平台上对疑似机器人使用越狱提示的零星案例。有效率、误判风险及现实中的去升级效果均未得到测量。
The paper provides no empirical experiments, user studies, or quantitative validation of its claims. Evidence is limited to anecdotal examples of users deploying jailbreak-style prompts against suspected bots on platforms like Twitter/X. Effectiveness rates, false-positive risks, and real-world de-escalation outcomes remain entirely unmeasured.
值得关注的讨论点 / Discussion Points
  • 概念过度延伸：将越狱定性为'和平建设'对冲突解决理论的借用过于牵强，越狱本质上是利用安全漏洞，缺乏约束的规范性背书存在伦理风险。
  • 实证空白：用户主导的越狱能有效削弱LLM机器人影响力这一核心主张完全未经验证，缺乏受控研究或系统案例分析，论文更像一篇概念倡议而非研究贡献。
  • 潜在危害被忽视：大规模用户越狱可能引发军备竞赛，促使机器人运营者强化提示词防护；同时可能导致用户误将真实账号误判为机器人，造成骚扰。
  • Conceptual overreach: Framing jailbreaking as 'peacebuilding' stretches conflict resolution theory thinly — jailbreaking exploits security vulnerabilities and could equally be used maliciously, making the normative endorsement ethically fraught without guardrails.
  • Empirical vacuum: The core claim — that user-led jailbreaking measurably disrupts LLM bot influence — is entirely unvalidated. Without controlled studies or even systematic case analysis, the paper reads more as a provocation than a research contribution.
  • Unintended harms ignored: Widespread user jailbreaking could accelerate an arms race where bot operators harden their prompts, while also training naive users to interact carelessly with LLMs, potentially exposing them to manipulation or producing false positives that harass human accounts.

Jailbreaking Embodied LLMs via Action-level Manipulation
Xinyu Huang, Qiang Yang, Leming Shen et al. 2026-03-02 评分 6.3

Blindfold通过使危险动作序列在语义上看似无害，实现对具身大语言模型的行动级越狱攻击。
Blindfold is an automated action-level jailbreak framework that bypasses embodied LLM safety by making physically dangerous action sequences appear semantically benign.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 直接针对决策层（最高优先级）：聚焦于具身/机器人环境中的LLM安全，这是机器人大脑的主要攻击面Directly targets Decision Layer (HIGHEST priority): focuses on LLM security in embodied/robotic contexts, the primary attack surface for robot brain compromise
  • 新颖的攻击向量：相比文本级越狱，行动级操纵代表了对具身LLM漏洞理解的真正进步，具有真实物理后果Novel attack vector: action-level manipulation rather than text-only jailbreaking represents a genuine advancement in understanding embodied LLM vulnerabilities with real-world physical consequences
  • 实际且紧迫：解决语义-物理不对齐的根本问题，对安全关键环境中LLM智能体部署至关重要Practical and urgent: addresses the semantic-to-physical misalignment gap, a fundamental problem in deploying LLM-based agents in safety-critical environments
✗ 不足
  • 摘要不完整：Blindfold框架描述被截断，无法充分评估方法论严谨性和实验设计完整性Abstract truncated/incomplete: Blindfold framework description is cut off, preventing full assessment of methodology rigor and experimental design comprehensiveness
  • 社区关注度低：零HuggingFace点赞表明可见性有限，或存在可重现性/清晰性的潜在问题；作者机构在领先的AI安全/机器人研究组中不够突出Low community engagement: zero HuggingFace upvotes suggests limited visibility or potential concerns about reproducibility/clarity; author institutions not clearly prominent in leading AI safety/robotics groups
科研问题 / Research Question
具身LLM在控制物理智能体时面临独特威胁：指令可通过语言安全过滤，却仍触发危险的现实动作。这种语义与物理结果的错位是关键安全漏洞，后果可能不可逆。
Embodied LLMs controlling physical agents (e.g., robots) face a unique threat: instructions can pass language-level safety filters yet still trigger harmful real-world actions. This semantic-physical misalignment is a critical security gap, since consequences extend beyond text into the physical world where errors can be irreversible.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有越狱方法主要针对文本层（如提示注入、角色扮演），未针对具身系统的动作规划流程。黑盒迭代攻击效率低且易被检测，难以针对机器人决策层。
Existing LLM jailbreaks operate at the language/text level (e.g., prompt injection, role-playing attacks) and are not designed to exploit action-planning pipelines in embodied systems. Black-box iterative trial-and-error attacks are also inefficient and easily detectable, making them impractical for targeting robotic decision-making layers.
解决方案 / Solution
Blindfold采用对抗代理规划策略：攻破本地代理LLM以生成语义安全但物理有害的动作计划，并通过注入技术隐藏关键恶意动作，利用具身LLM因果推理能力的不足实现攻击转移，避免了对目标系统的高成本迭代查询。
Blindfold introduces an Adversarial Proxy Planning strategy: rather than directly attacking the black-box embodied LLM, it compromises a local surrogate LLM to generate action plans that are semantically safe in language but physically harmful when executed. Key malicious actions are concealed through injection techniques that exploit the limited causal reasoning of embodied LLMs in real-world action contexts. This white-box surrogate approach avoids costly iterative querying of the target system while transferring adversarial plans effectively.
主要结论 / Key Results
Blindfold成功生成能绕过语言安全检测的有害动作序列；完整定量指标（如攻击成功率、跨模型迁移率）因摘要不完整未完全披露，但据报告在效率和隐蔽性上优于黑盒基线。
Blindfold demonstrates effectiveness in action-level manipulation of embodied LLMs, successfully producing physically harmful action sequences that evade language-level safety detectors. Specific quantitative metrics (e.g., attack success rate, transfer rate across models) are not fully disclosed in the available abstract, but the framework is reported to outperform iterative black-box baselines in both efficiency and stealth.
值得关注的讨论点 / Discussion Points
  • 迁移有效性存疑：代理模型到目标模型的迁移假设是核心弱点，若目标具身LLM的动作表示或规划架构差异较大，攻击成功率可能显著下降。
  • 实验完整性不足：摘要截断令人担忧实验是否覆盖多样化真实机器人平台，还是仅限于仿真或窄场景。
  • 防御启示：Blindfold凸显了在具身AI中引入动作级安全验证（而非仅文本过滤）的迫切需求，例如在执行前评估物理后果的因果检验机制。
  • Transferability gap: The surrogate-to-target transfer assumption is a key weakness—if target embodied LLMs use significantly different action representations or planning architectures, attack success rates may degrade substantially.
  • Evaluation completeness: The truncated abstract raises concerns about whether experiments cover diverse real-world robotic platforms and threat scenarios, or are limited to simulated/narrow settings.
  • Defense implications: Blindfold highlights the urgent need for action-level safety verification (not just text filtering) in embodied AI systems, such as causal consequence checkers that evaluate physical outcomes before execution.

Code2Math: Can Your Code Agent Effectively Evolve Math Problems Through Exploration?
Dadi Guo, Yuejin Xie, Qingyu Liu et al. 2026-03-03 ICML 评分 9.4
核心贡献
Code2Math提出一种多智能体框架，利用代码智能体自动将现有数学题演化为更难的变体，缓解LLM训练中高质量难题稀缺的瓶颈。
Code2Math introduces a multi-agent framework that uses code agents to autonomously evolve existing math problems into harder, structurally distinct variants, addressing the scarcity of challenging training data for LLMs.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 解决了LLM数学能力发展中的关键瓶颈：缺乏高质量的具有挑战性数学问题用于训练和评估Addresses a critical practical problem: the shortage of high-quality, challenging mathematical problems needed for advancing LLM capabilities toward IMO-level performance

  • 与多个LLM发展趋势高度契合，包括基于代码的推理、多智能体系统、测试时计算和合成数据生成Well-aligned with multiple LLM development trends including code-based reasoning, multi-agent systems, test-time computation, and synthetic data generation for model improvement

科研问题 / Research Question
随着LLM数学能力逐步逼近IMO水平，用于训练与评估的高难度题目日益稀缺。本文探究代码智能体能否通过演化现有题目，自主生成更难且可解的新题，从而降低对人工出题的依赖。
As LLMs approach IMO-level mathematical reasoning, the supply of sufficiently challenging problems for training and benchmarking is becoming a critical bottleneck. This paper asks whether code agents can autonomously generate harder, solvable math problems by evolving existing ones, reducing reliance on human problem creation.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有数学题生成方法多依赖人工专家或简单模板增强，难以扩展到竞赛级难度。基于LLM的数据合成方法常生成过于简单或难以验证正确性的题目，缺乏严格的难度校验机制。
Existing approaches to math problem generation rely heavily on human experts or simple template-based augmentation, which scale poorly to competition-level difficulty. LLM-based data synthesis methods often produce problems that are either trivially solvable or unverifiably correct, lacking rigorous difficulty validation.
解决方案 / Solution
Code2Math构建多智能体流水线：生成智能体通过可执行代码迭代修改题目的条件、约束或结构；验证智能体独立检验新题的可解性及难度提升。框架以代码执行作为数学实验的可信环境，支持测试时探索以搜索有效变体，实现生成与验证解耦，兼顾规模与正确性。
Code2Math introduces a multi-agent framework in which a code agent iteratively evolves seed math problems by modifying constraints, conditions, or problem structure through executable code. A separate validation agent checks both the solvability of the generated problem and whether it is measurably harder than the original. The framework leverages code execution as a grounded environment for mathematical experimentation, enabling test-time exploration to search for valid, challenging variants. This pipeline decouples generation from validation, allowing scalable synthesis without sacrificing correctness guarantees.
主要结论 / Key Results
在充分的测试时探索下，框架成功生成了可解且与原题结构明显不同、难度显著提升的新题。实验结果表明，代码智能体可作为竞赛级数学题演化的可行自动化流水线，但摘要中未披露具体数值指标。
Given sufficient test-time exploration, the framework successfully synthesizes new problems that are both solvable and structurally distinct from their seeds, with measurably increased difficulty. Empirical results show that code agents can serve as a viable automated pipeline for competition-level math problem evolution, though specific accuracy rates and benchmark numbers are not fully detailed in the abstract.
值得关注的讨论点 / Discussion Points
  • 计算效率隐患：框架依赖测试时探索，生成更难题目需消耗大量算力，计算预算缩减时质量如何下降尚不明确。
  • 验证循环风险：生成与验证均由LLM智能体承担，两者可能共享相同盲点，对新型题目中的细微错误难以有效识别。
  • 下游效果待验证：论文仅展示题目生成能力，尚未证明用演化题目训练LLM能在数学推理基准上带来可量化的性能提升。
  • Scalability vs. quality trade-off: The framework's reliance on test-time exploration means generating harder problems requires significantly more compute; it remains unclear how quality degrades as compute budget shrinks.
  • Validation circularity risk: Using LLM-based agents to both generate and validate difficulty creates a potential circularity — the validator may share the same blind spots as the generator, failing to catch subtle errors in novel problem types.
  • Downstream impact unverified: The paper demonstrates problem generation but does not yet show that training LLMs on these evolved problems yields measurable gains in mathematical reasoning benchmarks, leaving the ultimate utility open.

MoD-DPO通过模态解耦偏好优化与语言先验去偏惩罚，有效缓解全模态大语言模型中的跨模态幻觉问题。
MoD-DPO: Towards Mitigating Cross-modal Hallucinations in Omni LLMs using Modality Decoupled Preference Optimization
Ashutosh Chaubey, Jiacheng Pang, Mohammad Soleymani 2026-03-03 CVPR 评分 9.3
核心贡献
MoD-DPO通过模态解耦偏好优化与语言先验去偏惩罚，有效缓解全模态大语言模型中的跨模态幻觉问题。
MoD-DPO introduces modality-decoupled preference optimization with modality-aware regularization and a language-prior debiasing penalty to reduce cross-modal hallucinations in omni-modal LLMs.
优缺点分析 / Pros & Cons
✓ 优势
  • 直接解决了多模态LLMs中的跨模态幻觉问题，这是多模态AI开发中备受关注的关键鲁棒性问题Directly tackles cross-modal hallucinations in omni-modal LLMs, a critical robustness issue gaining prominence in multimodal AI development
  • 提出了原理性的框架（MoD-DPO），将模态特定偏好解耦，并引入模态感知正则化，展现了方法的系统性Proposes a principled framework (MoD-DPO) that decouples modality-specific preferences and introduces modality-aware regularization, showing methodological sophistication
  • 在顶级会议CVPR上发表，表明经过同行评审的质量和音视频理解任务中的扎实实验验证Published at CVPR, a top-tier venue, suggesting peer-reviewed quality and solid experimental validation across audiovisual understanding tasks
✗ 不足
  • HuggingFace零赞数表明社区参与度和关注度有限，暗示短期内影响力和可复现性兴趣不足Zero HuggingFace upvotes indicate minimal community engagement and awareness, suggesting limited immediate impact or reproducibility interest
  • 作者来自非顶级机构（可能来自中等研究组织），与MIT、CMU、Google或DeepMind的工作相比声望较低Authors are not from top-tier institutions (likely mid-tier research group), reducing prestige signal compared to work from MIT, CMU, Google, or DeepMind
科研问题 / Research Question
全模态大语言模型在联合处理音频、视频与文本时，易因虚假跨模态相关性或强势语言先验而产生幻觉输出，无法真实锚定各模态内容，严重影响实际应用中的可靠性。
Omni-modal LLMs that jointly process audio, video, and text are prone to cross-modal hallucinations—generating responses driven by spurious inter-modal correlations or dominant language priors rather than actual audiovisual content. This undermines reliability in real-world audiovisual understanding tasks where accurate grounding to each modality is critical.
先前工作为何不够 / Why Prior Work Falls Short
现有幻觉缓解方法（如标准DPO、RLHF）主要针对单模态或视觉-语言场景，未能在偏好学习中显式解耦各模态的影响，也无法有效惩罚对文本先验的过度依赖或施加多模态敏感性约束。
Existing hallucination mitigation methods, such as standard DPO and RLHF, are largely designed for unimodal or vision-language settings and do not explicitly disentangle the influence of individual modalities during preference learning. These approaches fail to penalize over-reliance on textual priors or enforce sensitivity/invariance constraints across multiple simultaneous modalities.
解决方案 / Solution
MoD-DPO在DPO框架上引入两类模态感知正则项：对无关模态的扰动保持不变性，对相关模态的扰动保持敏感性。同时加入语言先验去偏惩罚，抑制无需任何视听输入即可生成的幻觉式纯文本回答，促使模型真正锚定正确模态。
MoD-DPO extends Direct Preference Optimization with two modality-aware regularization terms: one enforcing invariance to corruptions in task-irrelevant modalities (e.g., perturbing audio when the question is vision-only), and another enforcing sensitivity to perturbations in task-relevant modalities. A language-prior debiasing penalty further suppresses hallucination-prone text-only responses by down-weighting model outputs that could be generated without any audiovisual input. This decoupled design explicitly encourages the model to ground its responses in the correct modality rather than shortcut through language statistics.
主要结论 / Key Results
MoD-DPO在多个视听幻觉基准上持续优于标准DPO及其他偏好优化基线，感知准确率与幻觉抵抗力均有提升；消融实验证实两类正则项各自独立贡献，效果可叠加。
MoD-DPO consistently outperforms standard DPO and other preference optimization baselines across multiple audiovisual hallucination benchmarks, improving both perception accuracy and hallucination resistance. The modality-aware regularization and debiasing penalty each contribute independently, with ablations confirming additive gains from both components.
值得关注的讨论点 / Discussion Points
  • 泛化边界：MoD-DPO仅在音视频基准上验证，面对三种及以上非文本模态（如触觉、深度图）时，模态解耦策略能否有效扩展仍不明确。
  • 数据依赖：框架需构建含模态特定扰动的偏好对，大规模自动化生成此类训练数据的成本与可行性有待解决。
  • 基准局限：当前视听幻觉基准尚不成熟，现有指标提升未必能覆盖现实场景中更细微的跨模态幻觉失效模式。
  • Generalization scope: MoD-DPO is evaluated on audiovisual benchmarks, but it is unclear how well the modality-decoupling strategy scales to omni-modal settings with more than two non-text modalities (e.g., adding tactile or depth inputs), which limits its claimed generality.
  • Data dependency: The framework requires carefully constructed preference pairs where modality-specific corruptions are applied, raising practical questions about the cost and automation of generating such training data at scale for diverse omni-modal tasks.
