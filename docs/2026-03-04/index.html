Existing Sparse Autoencoder (SAE) approaches predominantly operate at the token level, which creates a granularity mismatch when the goal is to understand reasoning steps rather than individual tokens. This limits their ability to disentangle high-level concepts like reasoning direction, error correction, or topic transitions that span entire steps in a CoT sequence.
解决方案 / Solution

SSAE introduces a step-level sparse autoencoder that encodes each reasoning step as a sparse feature vector conditioned on its preceding context. A key design is an information bottleneck mechanism: by controlling sparsity at the step level, SSAE separates "incremental information" (what is new and specific to a step) from "background information" (shared context), then disentangles the incremental part into sparsely activated dimensions. This enables fine-grained decomposition of what each reasoning step contributes to the overall solution.

  • 信息瓶颈设计假设上下文条件稀疏度能清晰分离增量与背景信息，该假设需要更充分的消融实验加以验证。
  • 目前社区关注度有限，未来研究应通过激活修补等干预手段验证SSAE特征与推理行为的因果关系，而非仅停留于相关性。

  • The information bottleneck design (splitting incremental vs. background information) is an elegant inductive bias, but it relies on the assumption that context-conditioned sparsity cleanly separates these two types of information — a claim that warrants deeper ablation studies.
  • Early-stage community uptake suggests the work is novel but not yet stress-tested; future work should evaluate whether SSAE features are causally relevant (e.g., via activation patching) rather than merely correlational with reasoning behavior.
